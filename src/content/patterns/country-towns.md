### Problem

The big city is a magnet. It is terribly hard for small towns to stay alive and healthy in the face of central urban growth.

### Solution

Preserve country towns where they exist; and encourage the growth of new self-contained towns, with populations between 500 and 10,000, entirely surrounded by open countryside and at least 10 miles from neighboring towns. Make it the region's collective concern to give to each town the wherewithal it needs to build a base of local industry, so that these towns are not dormitories for people who work in other places, but real townsâ€”able to sustain the whole of life.
