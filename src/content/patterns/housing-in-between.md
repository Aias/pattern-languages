### Problem

Wherever there is a sharp separation between residential and nonresidential parts of town, the nonresidential areas will quickly turn to slums.

### Solution

Build houses into the fabric of shops, small industry, schools, public services, universitiesâ€”all those parts of cities which draw people in during the day, but which tend to be "nonresidential". The houses may be in rows or "hills" with shops beneath, or they may be free-standing, so long as they mix with the other functions, and make the entire area "lived-in".
